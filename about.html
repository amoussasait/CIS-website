About CISS

Governing Islamic education with excellence, transparency, and community partnership

Who We Are

The Calgary Islamic School Society (CISS) is the governing body overseeing the Islamic School of Calgary. We provide strategic direction, governance oversight, and support for our two established campuses while planning for future expansion across Calgary.

CISS is committed to delivering excellent Islamic education that combines academic achievement with authentic Islamic values, preparing students to be confident, compassionate leaders in their communities.

Governance & Oversight

Our Board of Directors brings together diverse expertise in education, business, healthcare, and community leadership to ensure:

  • Strategic planning and long-term vision for Islamic education in Calgary
  • Financial stewardship and responsible resource management
  • Policy development and compliance with educational standards
  • Quality assurance across all programs and campuses
  • Accountability to families, students, and the broader community

Strategic Growth Planning

As Calgary's Muslim community continues to grow, CISS is actively planning for expansion:

  • Third Campus: Planning is underway to establish a third school campus to serve more families across Calgary
  • Online Education: Exploring online and hybrid learning options to increase accessibility (plans only, not yet launched)
  • Facility Enhancement: Ongoing improvements to existing campuses to support modern learning environments
  • Program Expansion: Developing new programs and extracurricular opportunities
Learn About Future Expansion

Transparency & Accountability

CISS operates with a commitment to transparency and open communication. We regularly share updates about governance decisions, financial health, strategic initiatives, and school performance with our community.

Parents and community members are encouraged to attend board meetings, review our policies, and engage with leadership through our comprehensive communication channels.

Excellence in Communication

We believe that strong partnerships between schools, parents, and community are essential for student success. CISS prioritizes:

  • Regular updates through multiple channels (email, website, newsletters)
  • Responsive support for parent questions and concerns
  • Clear policies and procedures accessible to all families
  • Opportunities for parent involvement and feedback

Our Mission

Calgary Islamic School strives to develop an enriched learning community that promotes academic achievement, leadership, and Islamic values.

Our Vision

To build and maintain a learning community that produces leaders through faith, knowledge, and ingenuity.

Our Beliefs

We believe that education is a shared responsibility between the school, parents, and the community, and that Islamic principles guide moral character, personal development, and lifelong learning.
